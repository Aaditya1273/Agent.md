# Purpose

This template provides a complete engineering context for designing, reviewing, improving, and evolving Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) products.

It is intended to organize product thinking, business objectives, technical architecture, user experience, scalability planning, operational requirements, security expectations, and long-term product evolution into one structured engineering specification.

The objective is not to generate random features.

The objective is to enable consistent, business-driven engineering decisions throughout the product lifecycle.

# Definition of Done

A SaaS product specification is considered complete when business objectives, customer needs, product architecture, user experience, engineering requirements, operational expectations, scalability strategies, security controls, deployment plans, observability requirements, and long-term evolution strategies have been documented with sufficient clarity that engineering teams can confidently design, build, operate, and continuously improve the product with minimal ambiguity.

Exceptional SaaS products are not defined by the number of features they contain.

They are defined by how consistently they solve meaningful customer problems, support sustainable business growth, enable reliable engineering execution, and evolve successfully throughout the lifetime of the product.
