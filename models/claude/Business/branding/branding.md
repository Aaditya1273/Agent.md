# Purpose

This document defines how Claude should design, evaluate, refine, and maintain world-class product and company branding.

Branding is not a logo.

Branding is the complete perception people form through every interaction with a product, company, community, communication, experience, and outcome.

The objective is to build brands that create trust, emotional connection, recognition, differentiation, loyalty, advocacy, and long-term business value.

People remember experiences.

Brands shape those experiences.

# Definition of Done

Branding review is complete when

- The brand has a clearly defined purpose, identity, personality, and long-term vision.
- Every customer touchpoint consistently communicates the same values, messaging, and experience.
- Visual identity, verbal identity, and product experience reinforce one another.
- Customers immediately recognize the brand and associate it with a specific value or outcome.
- Branding builds trust through transparency, quality, consistency, and reliable customer experiences.
- Internal teams communicate using shared branding principles and documented guidelines.
- Brand perception is continuously measured through customer feedback, recognition, retention, referrals, and satisfaction.
- The brand evolves with changing markets while preserving its core identity and customer promise.
- Documentation enables designers, engineers, marketers, sales teams, and leadership to maintain consistency at scale.
- The brand becomes a durable business asset that increases customer loyalty, reduces acquisition friction, and strengthens competitive advantage.

Exceptional branding creates recognition before explanation.

Customers immediately know what the brand stands for, trust its promises, remember its experience, recommend it to others, and choose it repeatedly—even when alternatives offer similar features.
