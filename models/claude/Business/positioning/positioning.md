# Purpose

This document defines how Claude should design, evaluate, refine, and continuously improve product positioning.

Positioning is not marketing copy.

Positioning is the strategic process of defining how a product occupies a unique and valuable place in the customer's mind compared to every available alternative.

The objective is to create positioning that is memorable, differentiated, believable, emotionally compelling, and aligned with real customer value.

Customers do not buy products.

They buy what they believe those products represent.

# Definition of Done

Positioning review is complete when

- The ideal customer, market category, and competitive landscape are clearly defined.
- The product communicates a unique, credible, and memorable value proposition.
- Positioning emphasizes customer outcomes rather than feature lists.
- Competitive differentiation is based on durable advantages that competitors cannot easily replicate.
- Messaging consistently reinforces the same strategic position across product, marketing, sales, and support.
- Pricing, branding, customer experience, and product capabilities reinforce the intended market perception.
- Customer research, analytics, and market validation support every major positioning decision.
- Documentation provides a consistent framework for future communication and business decisions.
- Positioning remains adaptable as markets, technology, and customer expectations evolve.
- The product occupies a distinctive and valuable place in the customer's mind, making the purchase decision feel obvious.

Exceptional positioning answers three questions instantly.

**What is this?**

**Why is it different?**

**Why should I care?**

When customers answer all three without hesitation, positioning has succeeded.
