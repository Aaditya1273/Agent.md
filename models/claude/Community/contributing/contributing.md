# Purpose

This document defines the contribution standards, expectations, workflows, and review process for everyone contributing to this repository.

A contribution is more than adding content.

Every contribution should improve the repository's quality, consistency, maintainability, and long-term value.

The goal is not to accept more contributions.

The goal is to accept better contributions.

# Definition of Done

A contribution is complete when

- The problem has been clearly identified and addressed with a well-structured, accurate, and maintainable solution.
- Repository standards for structure, formatting, terminology, and documentation have been consistently followed.
- The contribution improves clarity, usability, organization, or long-term maintainability without introducing unnecessary complexity.
- Technical information has been verified, assumptions are documented, and content is suitable for long-term reference.
- Self-review has eliminated formatting issues, inconsistencies, duplication, and avoidable mistakes before submission.
- Review feedback has been addressed respectfully, transparently, and constructively.
- The contribution integrates naturally with existing documentation and preserves a consistent reader experience across the repository.
- Future contributors can easily understand, maintain, and extend the work without additional explanation.
- The repository becomes more valuable, more reliable, and easier to maintain because of the contribution.
- The contribution demonstrates professionalism, collaboration, continuous learning, and a commitment to long-term repository excellence.

Exceptional repositories are not built by individual commits.

They are built through thousands of thoughtful contributions that consistently improve quality, preserve knowledge, strengthen collaboration, and leave the project better than it was before.
